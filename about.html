About the Z-Shift Computation Tool

The Z-Shift Computation Tool is designed to assist researchers and practitioners in analyzing transition matrices, particularly in the context of credit ratings and economic models. The tool computes the z-shift between observed transition matrices and a stable (neutral) matrix, providing insights into shifts in probabilities across different states.

Features:

  • Customizable number of states (rows and columns).
  • Option to input your own stable matrix or compute it automatically.
  • Ability to input multiple observed matrices.
  • Accurate z-shift computations using robust algorithms.
  • Visual representation of results through interactive charts.
  • Option to export results for further analysis.

Computation Methodology:
The z-shift computation involves adjusting the stable matrix to minimize the difference between the adjusted stable matrix and the observed matrices. This is achieved by applying a scalar shift to the log-odds of the stable matrix probabilities and optimizing this shift to minimize the sum of squared differences.

Data Privacy:
All computations are performed locally in your browser. No data is transmitted to any server, ensuring your data remains confidential.
